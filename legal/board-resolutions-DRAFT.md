# DRAFT — Organizational Board Resolutions of Walk Through Labs

*Unanimous written consent of the Board of Directors in lieu of an organizational meeting. **DRAFT for attorney review — not executed.***

The undersigned, constituting all of the directors of Walk Through Labs, a Utah nonprofit corporation (the "Corporation"), hereby consent to and adopt the following resolutions:

**1. Ratification of incorporation.** RESOLVED, that the filing of the Articles of Incorporation with the Utah Division of Corporations and Commercial Code is ratified and approved.

**2. Bylaws.** RESOLVED, that the bylaws presented to the Board are adopted as the bylaws of the Corporation.

**3. Officers.** RESOLVED, that the following persons are elected to serve until the first annual meeting: President — ______________; Secretary — ______________; Treasurer — ______________.

**4. Conflict of interest policy.** RESOLVED, that the Conflict of Interest Policy presented to the Board is adopted, and each director and officer shall execute the annual disclosure statement.

**5. EIN, bank account, fiscal year.** RESOLVED, that the officers are authorized to obtain an EIN, open depository accounts, and that the fiscal year shall end December 31.

**6. Form 1023.** RESOLVED, that the officers are authorized and directed to prepare and file IRS Form 1023 seeking recognition of exemption under Section 501(c)(3), and to pay the associated user fee, and to make any state charitable-registration and tax-exemption filings.

**7. Acceptance of asset contribution.** RESOLVED, that the Corporation accepts the contribution of intellectual property and related assets from [GullStack entity] described in the Intellectual Property Assignment and Asset Transfer Agreement presented to the Board, having determined that (a) the assets further the Corporation's exempt purposes, (b) the transfer terms are fair and reasonable to the Corporation, and (c) any director with an interest in the assignor disclosed that interest and was recused from deliberation and vote in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy; and the disinterested directors are authorized to execute the agreement.

**8. Program policies.** RESOLVED, that the officers shall present for adoption at the first regular meeting: (a) a qualification and sliding-scale fee policy for remediation assistance; (b) a document-retention policy; and (c) a whistleblower policy.
