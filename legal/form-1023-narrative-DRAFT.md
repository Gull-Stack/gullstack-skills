Walk Through Labs (the "Organization") is a Utah nonprofit corporation organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3). Its mission is to protect small businesses and the public from malicious and deceptive uses of artificial intelligence, including AI-enabled fraud, impersonation, fake or scam websites, and manipulation of businesses' online presence in search engines and AI answer engines.

## Activity 1 — Education and published materials (approx. 40% of effort)

The Organization researches, writes, and publishes free educational materials teaching business owners how to establish and defend a truthful, verifiable online presence against AI-enabled deception. These include step-by-step playbooks on website authenticity signals, structured data and machine-readable brand facts (so AI systems cite the real business rather than impostors), fraud-resistant contact and payment flows, and detection of impersonation sites. Materials are published openly at no charge on the Organization's website and public code repositories, and are usable by any business or practitioner. The Organization also conducts free workshops and webinars for small-business owners and local business associations.

**How this furthers exempt purposes:** instruction of the public on subjects useful to individuals and beneficial to the community (Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(3)); relief of distress for defrauded small businesses; lessening the burdens of consumer-protection efforts.

## Activity 2 — Free and low-cost defensive tools (approx. 30% of effort)

The Organization develops and distributes software tools that help businesses detect and defend against AI-driven harms — for example, monitoring for cloned or impersonating websites, verifying that AI answer engines return accurate information about a business, and publishing machine-readable authenticity records. Tools are released without charge or at nominal cost under open licenses.

## Activity 3 — Direct remediation assistance (approx. 25% of effort)

The Organization provides hands-on help to businesses that have been harmed by AI-enabled fraud or deception — e.g., a business impersonated by a scam website, defamed by AI-generated content, or displaced in AI search results by fraudulent actors. Assistance is provided free of charge to qualifying small businesses, with eligibility based on business size and financial need under a written policy adopted by the Board; a sliding-scale cost-recovery fee may apply to businesses above the qualification thresholds, set below market rates and applied uniformly.

## Activity 4 — Research (approx. 5% of effort)

The Organization documents patterns of AI-enabled fraud affecting small businesses and publishes findings publicly to inform business owners, researchers, and consumer-protection agencies.

## Fundraising and revenue

The Organization will be funded by public donations, grants from foundations and government programs, and program-service revenue from the sliding-scale fees described in Activity 3. The Organization received an initial contribution of intellectual property (software, educational materials, and related assets) from its founder's predecessor business, as described in Part [X] and the attached asset list and appraisal. Any activity generating unrelated business income will be tracked and reported on Form 990-T; the Board's policy is to keep unrelated activity insubstantial or house it in a taxable subsidiary.

## Governance

The Organization is governed by a board of at least three directors, a majority of whom are independent. It has adopted a conflict-of-interest policy following the IRS sample, and all insider compensation and transactions follow the rebuttable-presumption procedure of Section 4958 (advance approval by disinterested directors, comparability data, contemporaneous documentation).
