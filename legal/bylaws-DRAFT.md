# DRAFT — Bylaws of Walk Through Labs

*A Utah nonprofit corporation. **DRAFT for attorney review — not adopted.***

## Article 1 — Name, Purpose, Offices

1.1 The name of the corporation is Walk Through Labs (the "Corporation").
1.2 The Corporation's purposes are as stated in its Articles of Incorporation: exclusively charitable, educational, and scientific purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, centered on protecting businesses from malicious and deceptive uses of artificial intelligence.
1.3 The principal office shall be located at the address designated by the Board of Directors.

## Article 2 — No Members

2.1 The Corporation shall have no voting members. All corporate powers are exercised by or under the authority of the Board of Directors.

## Article 3 — Board of Directors

3.1 **Powers.** The Board manages the affairs, property, and business of the Corporation.
3.2 **Number.** The Board shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than nine (9) directors. A majority of directors shall at all times be independent — not related to one another or to any officer by family or controlling business interest.
3.3 **Term.** Directors serve staggered terms of three (3) years and may serve successive terms.
3.4 **Election and removal.** Directors are elected by majority vote of the Board. A director may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds vote of the other directors.
3.5 **Vacancies.** Vacancies are filled by majority vote of the remaining directors.
3.6 **Compensation.** Directors serve without compensation for board service, but may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses. Nothing prevents a director from serving the Corporation in another capacity and receiving reasonable compensation therefor, subject to the Conflict of Interest Policy.

## Article 4 — Meetings

4.1 **Annual meeting.** The Board shall hold an annual meeting to elect officers and directors and to review the budget, programs, and audit/financial review.
4.2 **Regular and special meetings.** Regular meetings are held as scheduled by the Board (no fewer than quarterly). Special meetings may be called by the President or any two directors on at least 48 hours' notice.
4.3 **Quorum; action.** A majority of directors in office constitutes a quorum. The act of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum exists is the act of the Board, except where these bylaws or law require a greater proportion.
4.4 **Remote participation; written consent.** Directors may participate by any means of communication by which all participants can hear each other. Action may be taken without a meeting by unanimous written consent.
4.5 **Minutes.** Minutes shall be kept for every meeting and retained permanently.

## Article 5 — Officers

5.1 The officers shall be a President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer, elected annually by the Board. One person may hold two offices, except President and Secretary.
5.2 **President** — presides at Board meetings; general supervision of the Corporation's affairs subject to Board direction.
5.3 **Secretary** — notices, minutes, corporate records.
5.4 **Treasurer** — custody of funds; books of account; financial reports at each regular meeting; annual filings (IRS Form 990 series, state renewals).

## Article 6 — Conflicts of Interest and Compensation Approval

6.1 The Corporation shall adopt and enforce a Conflict of Interest Policy (see `conflict-of-interest-policy-DRAFT.md`).
6.2 Compensation of officers, and any transaction between the Corporation and an insider (director, officer, key employee, substantial contributor, or entity any of them controls), must be approved in advance by disinterested members of the Board, relying on comparability data, with the interested person recused and the deliberation and basis documented contemporaneously in the minutes (the IRS "rebuttable presumption" procedure under Section 4958).

## Article 7 — Prohibited Activities

7.1 The Corporation shall not engage in activities prohibited to organizations exempt under Section 501(c)(3), including private inurement, more-than-insubstantial lobbying, and any political campaign intervention.
7.2 Any unrelated business activity shall be monitored by the Treasurer; if unrelated business income becomes more than insubstantial, the Board shall consult counsel regarding restructuring (including use of a taxable subsidiary).

## Article 8 — Financial Administration

8.1 Fiscal year: January 1 – December 31.
8.2 Two-signature (or dual electronic approval) requirement for disbursements above a threshold set annually by the Board.
8.3 The Board shall adopt an annual budget and review financial statements at each regular meeting.
8.4 Required records — governing documents, minutes, Form 1023 and determination letter, Forms 990, and financial statements — shall be retained and made available for public inspection as required by law.

## Article 9 — Indemnification

9.1 The Corporation shall indemnify directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by the Utah Revised Nonprofit Corporation Act and may purchase directors-and-officers liability insurance.

## Article 10 — Amendments

10.1 These bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds vote of the directors then in office at any meeting for which notice of the proposed amendment was given.
