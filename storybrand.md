# StoryBrand & Donald Miller Marketing Framework

**Source:** Donald Miller's *Building a StoryBrand*, *Marketing Made Simple*, *Business Made Simple*

---

## Core Philosophy

> "Pretty websites don't sell things. Words sell things."

> "If you confuse, you'll lose."

> "The customer is the hero, not your brand."

**The Two Mistakes Brands Make:**
1. They fail to focus on aspects that help people survive and thrive
2. They make customers burn too many calories to understand their offer

**The Grunt Test:** Could a caveman look at your website and immediately grunt what you offer? Within 5 seconds, visitors must answer:
- What do you offer?
- How will it make my life better?
- What do I need to do to buy it?

## The SB7 Framework (7-Part BrandScript)

Every story follows this arc. So should your marketing:

### 1. A CHARACTER (The Customer)
- The customer is the hero, NOT your brand
- Define ONE clear thing they want as it relates to your brand
- Tie it to survival/thriving: conserving resources, saving time, building social networks, gaining status, finding meaning

**Question:** What does your customer want?

### 2. HAS A PROBLEM
- The problem is the HOOK — without it, no one listens
- Three levels of problems:
  - **External:** The tangible, surface problem (ugly lawn, leaky pipe)
  - **Internal:** How it makes them FEEL (embarrassed, frustrated, overwhelmed)
  - **Philosophical:** Why it's WRONG (shouldn't have to struggle with this)

**The Villain:**
- Must be a root source (not just "frustration")
- Must be relatable
- Must be singular (one villain)
- Must be real (no fearmongering)

> "Companies sell solutions to external problems, but customers buy solutions to internal problems."

**Question:** What villain are you fighting? What external, internal, and philosophical problems does it cause?

### 3. AND MEETS A GUIDE (Your Brand)
- Position yourself as Yoda, not Luke Skywalker
- Brands that position as heroes compete with their customers

**Two Qualities of a Guide:**
1. **Empathy:** "We understand how it feels to..." / "Nobody should have to..."
2. **Authority:** Testimonials, statistics, awards, logos

**Question:** How do you show you understand their pain AND that you're competent to help?

### 4. WHO GIVES THEM A PLAN
- The plan alleviates confusion and fear
- 3-6 steps maximum

**Two Types of Plans:**
1. **Process Plan:** Steps to buy or use your product
   - "1. Schedule a call. 2. Get a custom plan. 3. See results."
2. **Agreement Plan:** Promises that overcome fears
   - Guarantees, quality commitments, values

**Give your plan a name:** "The Easy Installation Plan" / "Our Quality Guarantee"

**Question:** What simple steps do customers take to work with you?

### 5. AND CALLS THEM TO ACTION
- Customers don't act unless challenged
- Passive asks = weak sales = "we don't believe in our product"

**Two Types of CTAs:**
1. **Direct:** "Buy Now" / "Schedule Today" / "Register Now"
   - Make ONE button obvious, repeat it throughout the page
2. **Transitional:** Free value that builds trust
   - Free PDF, webinar, sample, trial, consultation

**Question:** What's your direct CTA? What transitional CTA builds trust?

### 6. THAT HELPS THEM AVOID FAILURE
- Every story needs stakes
- Use the fear appeal (sparingly, like salt):
  1. You're vulnerable to a threat
  2. You should take action
  3. Here's a specific action
  4. Do it now

**Question:** What negative outcome do you help them avoid?

### 7. AND ENDS IN SUCCESS
- NEVER assume they know how you'll change their life — TELL THEM
- Show the transformation

**Three Ways Stories End in Success:**
1. Win power or position
2. Unite with something that makes them whole
3. Experience self-realization

**Aspirational Identity:** Who does the customer want to BECOME?
- How do they want friends to describe them?
- "Competent, generous, disciplined, fit, successful"

**Question:** What does success look like? Who do they become?

## The One-Liner Formula

The elevator pitch. 3-4 sentences max.

**Structure:**
1. **Problem:** State the pain point (opens the story loop)
2. **Solution:** Your product/service (clear and direct)
3. **Success:** Paint the picture of their better life

**Template:**
> "[Most/Many] [customers] struggle with [problem]. [Brand name] [solution]. So you can [success/result]."

**Examples:**
- "Most parents stress about taking kids to the dentist. At Kid's Teeth, our fun office puts kids at ease, so they're not afraid and parents enjoy the visit."
- "Taking care of your lawn year-round feels like one more chore. With GroSure Lawncare, you'll never worry about your lawn again while you spend time doing what you love."
- "You're busy enough without worrying about your financial future. Partner with Fuller Financial and get a team of experts giving you peace of mind about your finances."

## The 5-Part Sales Funnel (Marketing Made Simple)

### The Three Relationship Stages
1. **Curiosity:** Snap decision — keep or discard? (Headline, subject line)
2. **Enlightenment:** Invite into relationship (Long-form content, lead gen)
3. **Commitment:** Ask for the sale (at the right time)

### The Five Components

#### 1. ONE-LINER
- See formula above
- Use everywhere: email signature, social bios, business cards, wall signage
- Train every employee to say it

#### 2. WEBSITE (Wireframe)
Nine sections (order flexible except header first):
1. **Header:** What you offer in few words + obvious CTA
2. **Stakes:** Pain and frustration you resolve
3. **Value Proposition:** List of benefits (3-5 bullets)
4. **The Guide:** Introduce your brand with empathy + authority
5. **The Plan:** 3-step process to work with you
6. **Explanatory Paragraph:** Long-form BrandScript (SEO)
7. **Video (optional):** Reiterate core message
8. **Price Choices (optional):** Tiers or divisions
9. **Junk Drawer:** Everything else (footer stuff)

**Above the fold MUST:**
- Promise an aspirational identity, OR
- Promise to solve a problem, OR
- State exactly what you do

**CTAs:** Top right + center of page. One obvious button, repeated throughout.

**Images:** Happy people using your product (show transformation)

#### 3. LEAD GENERATOR
Give something valuable in exchange for email:
- PDF guide, checklist, video series, free sample, quiz, webinar

**Great lead gen characteristics:**
- Positions you as guide
- Answers their questions
- Solves a real problem
- Piques interest
- Creates reciprocity
- Builds trust
- Challenges small action
- Shows vision of success

**Title formula:** Make it specific + outcome-focused
- "5 Mistakes People Make With Their First Million"
- "10 Things to Get Right Before You Build"
- "How to Get Your Dog to Stop Barking"

**Promote your lead gen like a product.** Spend more ad budget on lead gen than direct product ads.

#### 4. NURTURE EMAIL CAMPAIGN
Ongoing value emails (weekly minimum):
- Solve problems
- Offer valuable info/access
- Remind them your product helps
- Send back to website

**Formula:** 3 value emails → 1 soft CTA → repeat

**Keep it short:** Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Active language.

#### 5. SALES EMAIL CAMPAIGN
Focused sequence to close the deal (sent to new leads BEFORE nurture):

1. Deliver the lead magnet
2. Identify problem → empathize → introduce product
3. Customer testimonials
4. Overcome an objection
5. Paradigm shift (how you're different)
6. Direct ask for the sale

## Website Copy Principles

**Header:**
- Aspirational identity + clear offer
- "Financial Advisor: A Plan for Your Retirement"
- "Real Estate: The Home You've Dreamed About"

**Headlines should:**
- State what you offer clearly
- Promise to solve a problem
- Include a call to action

**Body copy:**
- Lead with the problem
- Position customer as hero
- You are the guide
- Give them a plan
- Call them to action
- Stakes: failure and success

**Kill corporate fluff:**
- No "we're committed to excellence"
- No "we're industry leaders"
- No "since 1987"
- Just: how you help them survive and thrive

## BrandScript Template

Fill this out for every brand/product/campaign:

| Element | Your Answer |
|---------|-------------|
| **Character (Customer)** | Who are they? What do they want? |
| **External Problem** | What tangible issue? |
| **Internal Problem** | How do they feel? |
| **Philosophical Problem** | Why is it wrong? |
| **The Villain** | What/who causes the problem? |
| **Guide (You)** | How do you show empathy? Authority? |
| **The Plan** | 3 steps to work with you |
| **Call to Action (Direct)** | "Buy Now" equivalent |
| **Call to Action (Transitional)** | Free value offer |
| **Avoid Failure** | What bad outcome do you prevent? |
| **Success** | What does their life look like after? |
| **Aspirational Identity** | Who do they become? |

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before publishing ANY marketing:

- [ ] Is the customer the hero?
- [ ] Is the problem clear (external + internal)?
- [ ] Are we positioned as guide, not hero?
- [ ] Is there a simple plan (3-6 steps)?
- [ ] Is there a clear, bold CTA?
- [ ] Are the stakes defined (failure + success)?
- [ ] Would a caveman understand this in 5 seconds?
- [ ] Does it help them survive or thrive?
- [ ] Is it simple enough to repeat?

## Applying This to GullStack Clients

For every client website/campaign:

1. **Start with BrandScript** — fill out the template
2. **Write the one-liner** — problem → solution → success
3. **Wireframe the homepage** — use the 9-section structure
4. **Create a lead gen** — PDF guide or free offer
5. **Set up email sequence** — sales emails first, then nurture
6. **Test the grunt test** — show someone for 5 seconds, ask what you do
